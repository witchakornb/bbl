The Knight's Tour.

A classic mathematical problem, visualized with precision and elegance. Scroll to uncover the logic.

Warnsdorff's Rule.

To solve the tour efficiently, the knight always chooses the next square that has the fewest onward moves. This simple yet profound heuristic elegantly avoids dead ends, guiding the piece through complex grids with mathematical grace.

The Center Constraint.

For a truly symmetrical journey, we enforce a strict condition on odd-sized boards: the knight must land exactly on the center square at the exact midpoint of its tour (step (n² + 1) / 2).
